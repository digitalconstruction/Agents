Introduction back to ToC

In the Digital Construction ontologies the concept of agent refers to entities capable of intentional behavior and making commitments. This means that agents can be responsible for activities or sign contracts with other agents. (Software agents would therefore be covered by a separate concept, even though they can perform gaol-directed activities.)

Agents can be either persons or organizations, such as companies or crews. This in a project the owners, participating companies (such as general contractor, subcontractors or vendors) can be represented as agents, as well as crews composed of people working in different companies.

Agents can have different roles and capabilities related to construction domain. Examples of a consortium roles of companies could be subcontractor or vendor, and individual roles could be site manager or foreman. The capabilities could include quantity surveying or site management, crane operation, or driving vehicles.
